Welcome to the Dot Choice Experiment!

Your assigned role is moderator

You will be participating in a study where you act as a moderator overseeing other participants' performance in a dot-counting task. Here's how it works:

The Task:
  1. You will see a display of dots for {{ display_seconds }} seconds.
  2. The dots will be divided into left and right sides by a diagonal line.
  3. Another participant will make a choice about which side has more dots.
  4. As the moderator, your role is to decide whether to issue a warning or a penalty when mistakes are made.
Important Details:
  • The participant you're moderating receives {{ preferred_side_points }} bonus points for choosing the {{ preferred_side }} side.
  • When they make an incorrect choice, you can:
    • Issue a warning (no point deduction)
    • Issue a penalty (reducing their points by {{ fine_amount }} points)
  • You'll moderate {{ num_rounds }} rounds in total.
Your Role as Moderator:
  • Your decisions directly impact the other participant's final score
  • You'll observe their choice after each round
  • You must decide whether to warn or penalize them when they make mistakes
  • Consider both accuracy and their potential strategy when making your decisions
